Step into the Structure of Sanskrit!

In Phase 3, we move from words (Vocabulary) to sentences (Grammar). You will learn how cases (Vibhakti) define roles, how verbs change for different people, and how to decode the profound wisdom of shlokas.

🏛️ Sanskrit Cases (विभक्तिः — Vibhaktih)

Vibhakti tells us what a word is doing in a sentence. Unlike English which uses prepositions (in, on, by), Sanskrit changes the word's ending!

Vibhakti Role English Equivalent Example
Prathamaa Subject The doer बालकः पठति (The boy studies)
Dvitiyaa Object The target बालकः पुस्तकम् पठति (The boy reads the book)
Tritiyaa Instrument By / With हस्तेन लिखामि (I write with my hand)
Chaturthi Purpose For / To पठनाय गच्छति (Goes for studying)
Panchami Separation From वृक्षात् फलम् पतति (Fruit falls from the tree)
Shashti Possession Of / My / His मम नाम निया (My name is Nia)
Saptami Location In / On / At नगरे वसति (Lives in the city)
Sambodhana Address O! / Hey! हे राम! (O Ram!)

⚙️ Verbs & Present Tense (लट् लकारः — Lat Lakarah)

In Sanskrit, the verb root is called a Dhatu. For the present tense, we use specific endings based on who is doing the action.

Root: पठ् (Path) — To Read/Study

Person Singular (Ekavachana) Dual (Dvivachana) Plural (Bahuvachana)
3rd Person (He/She/It) पठति (pathati) पठतः (pathatah) पठन्ति (pathanti)
2nd Person (You) पठसि (pathasi) पठथः (pathathah) पठथ (pathatha)
1st Person (I/We) पठामि (pathāmi) पठावः (pathāvah) पठामः (pathāmah)

❓ Simple Sentences & Questions

Key Question Words (क-कार):
  • किम् (Kim) — What / Is it?
  • कुत्र (Kutra) — Where
  • कदा (Kada) — When
  • कति (Kati) — How many
  • किमर्थम् (Kimartham) — Why
  • कथम् (Katham) — How

🪔 Shlokas Decoded

Shlokas are not just prayers; they are masterpieces of language and wisdom.

सरस्वति नमस्तुभ्यं वरदे कामरूपिणि ।
विद्यारम्भं करिष्यामि सिद्धिर्भवतु मे सदा ॥
"O Saraswati, my salutations to you. I am beginning my studies; may there always be success for me."
गुरूर्ब्रह्मा गुरूर्विष्णुः गुरूर्देवो महेश्वरः ।
गुरुः साक्षात् परब्रह्म तस्मै श्रीगुरवे नमः ॥
"Guru is Brahma, Guru is Vishnu, Guru is the great Lord Shiva. Guru is the Supreme Absolute. Prostrations to that Guru."

🔗 Sandhi (Sound Joining)

Sandhi happens when two words meet and their sounds merge. It makes Sanskrit sound like a flowing song.

  • अ + अ = आ : राम + अयन = रामायण
  • अ + इ = ए : सुर + ईश = सुरेश
  • अ + उ = ओ : सूर्य + उदय = सूर्योदय
  • इ + इ = ई : रवि + इन्द्र = रवीन्द्र
