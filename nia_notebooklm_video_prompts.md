
#### S1-04 · Sanskrit Consonants Part 1 — The Throat & Palate Sounds (Varga 1 & 2)

```
You are creating an educational video for Nia, a curious 10-year-old Indian girl strong in English, learning Sanskrit for the first time in Class 5 CBSE.

TONE: Curious and scientific. Frame the consonant system as an amazing discovery about how the human mouth works.

VIDEO STRUCTURE:
1. HOOK (1 min): "Sanskrit consonants are organised by science — not randomly like English! They are sorted by WHERE in your mouth you make the sound." This is the most organised alphabet in the world.
2. THE VARGA SYSTEM (1.5 min): Explain that Sanskrit has 25 consonants arranged in 5 rows of 5 called Vargas. Today: the first two rows — Kantha (throat sounds) and Talu (palate sounds).
3. VARGA 1 — KANTHA (throat) (2.5 min): क ख ग घ ङ
   - क (Ka) — like 'k' in 'kite' → कमल (Kamal = lotus)
   - ख (Kha) — aspirated k → खग (Khaga = bird)
   - ग (Ga) — like 'g' in 'go' → गणेश (Ganesh)
   - घ (Gha) — aspirated g → घर (Ghar = home — "Nia, you already know this word!")
   - ङ (Nga) — rare nasal, like 'ng' in 'sing'
   Teach the pattern: unaspirated → aspirated → voiced → voiced aspirated → nasal
4. VARGA 2 — TALU (palate) (2.5 min): च छ ज झ ञ
   - च (Cha) → चन्द्र (Chandra = moon)
   - छ (Chha) → छात्र (Chhaatra = student — "That's you, Nia!")
   - ज (Ja) → जल (Jal = water)
   - झ (Jha) → झरना (Jharna = waterfall)
   - ञ (Nya) — nasal palate sound
5. THE PATTERN MAGIC (1 min): Highlight the 5-pattern that repeats across all rows. "Once you see this pattern, learning the other 15 consonants becomes easy!"
6. NIA'S CHALLENGE: Say each of today's 10 consonants out loud, touching the part of the mouth where the sound comes from.
7. CLOSE: Preview next — Murdha (retroflex) and Danta (dental) sounds.

LANGUAGE RULE: English throughout. Each Sanskrit letter shown with pronunciation guide. Encourage Nia to say sounds aloud.
DURATION TARGET: 9–11 minutes.
```

#### S1-06 · Sanskrit Writing Practice — Matras, Conjunct Letters & Reading Your First Words

```
You are creating an educational video for Nia, a curious 10-year-old Indian girl strong in English, learning Sanskrit for the first time in Class 5 CBSE.

TONE: Hands-on and practical. This is Nia's first "I can actually READ Sanskrit!" moment. Make it feel like a superpower being unlocked.

2. WHAT ARE MATRAS? (2 min): Explain that when a vowel follows a consonant, it changes shape and becomes a matra (vowel sign) written above, below, or around the consonant. Show the matra chart for the 6 key vowels alongside the base consonant क.
   - क + आ → का (kaa)
   - क + इ → कि (ki)
   - क + उ → कु (ku)
   - क + ए → के (ke)

3. READING SIMPLE WORDS (3 min): Walk through reading these words letter by letter:
   - नमः (Namah) — decompose each letter
   - जलम् (Jalam = water)
   - रामः (Ramah — the name Ram in Sanskrit)
   - निया (Nia — show Nia her own name!)
4. CONJUNCT CONSONANTS — A PREVIEW (1.5 min): Explain that when two consonants come together with no vowel between them, they join (like स्क in Sanskrit = 'sk'). Don't go deep — just show 2 examples so Nia isn't shocked when she sees them.
5. NIA'S CHALLENGE: Write 5 names from her family in Devanagari. Sound them out, letter by letter.
6. CLOSE: "You have completed Phase 1 of Sanskrit! You know the entire alphabet, how sounds work, and how to read simple words. Phase 2 is all about actual Sanskrit words — and they're everywhere around you!"

LANGUAGE RULE: English explanations. Show each letter combination visually with a clear breakdown. Slow and deliberate pacing for this video — it's a skill-building session.
DURATION TARGET: 10–12 minutes.
```

#### S3-06 · Sanskrit — Sandhi (The Art of Sound Joining)

```
You are creating a grammar video for Nia, a 10-year-old girl learning Sanskrit, Class 5 CBSE.

TOPIC: Introduction to Sandhi — the Sanskrit system of sound joining.

VIDEO STRUCTURE:
1. HOOK: "In Sanskrit, when words sit next to each other, they don't stay separate — they JOIN and change! It's like when people are very comfortable with each other — they start speaking in shortcuts and combinations. Sanskrit does this systematically. This system is called Sandhi (सन्धि) — which means 'joining'."
2. WHY SANDHI? (1.5 min): Sanskrit values smooth sound flow. Two vowels or certain consonant pairs can't comfortably sit next to each other — so they merge into a new, smoother sound. Sandhi rules are the grammar of this merging.
3. VOWEL SANDHI — THE BASICS (3.5 min): The simplest and most common rules:
   - अ + अ = आ: Example: राम + अयन = रामायण (Rama + ayana = Ramayana!) Nia knows this word!
   - अ + इ = ए: Example: सूर्य + उदय → (the sun-rise sandhi)
   - इ + इ = ई: Example: मुनि + इन्द्र = मुनीन्द्र
4. SPOTTING SANDHI IN SHLOKAS (3 min): Go back to shlokas from previous videos and show where sandhi happened:
   - गुरुः + ब्रह्मा = गुरुर्ब्रह्मा
   - त्वम् + एव = त्वमेव
   - Show Nia that what looked like "long scary words" in shlokas are actually 2-3 smaller words joined by sandhi!
5. NIA'S CHALLENGE: Break apart 5 sandhi words from her textbook into their original components.

LANGUAGE NOTE: Explain that Nia doesn't need to master all sandhi rules now — the goal is to RECOGNISE sandhi so she isn't scared by long Sanskrit words.
DURATION TARGET: 9–11 minutes.
```
