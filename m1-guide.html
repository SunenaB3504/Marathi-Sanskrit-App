Welcome to the Marathi Letters Reference Guide!

This guide provides a complete overview of all Marathi letters, vowel marks (matras), and combined letters with examples. Marathi uses the Devanagari script but has its own unique characteristics. Use this as a quick reference while learning!

⭐ The Special Marathi Letter: ळ

What is ळ?
ळ

Romanized as: ḷa

The letter ळ (ḷa) is unique to Marathi! It doesn't exist in Sanskrit or Hindi. It's pronounced as a retroflex lateral sound, similar to "la" but with the tongue more curled back.

Examples:
• गुळ (Guḷ) = Jaggery
• फुळ (Phuḷ) = Flower
• मिळ (Miḷ) = Get/Obtain

🔗 Combined Letters & Tricks

Consonants can combine to form clusters. Here are useful patterns and tricks for Marathi.

Halant (्) — The Virama

What is Halant? The halant (also called virama) is a symbol (्) that removes the inherent vowel from a consonant, making it a pure consonant sound.

Example: क् (k without 'a') + त = क्त (kta)

💡 Marathi-Specific Tips

  • Watch for ळ: This unique Marathi letter appears in many everyday words
  • Nasal Sounds: Marathi uses anusvara (ं) and visarga (ः) frequently
  • Chandrabindu (ँ): Used for nasal vowels in some words
  • Marathi Pronunciation: Can differ from Sanskrit, especially in consonant clusters
  • Practice Distinction: Pay attention to single vs. double consonants (त vs. त्त)

⭐ Quick Reference Tips

🎯 Learning Order for Marathi

  • Start with vowels (अ से औ)
  • Learn consonants group by group (वर्ग)
  • Master the special letter ळ early
  • Practice matras with each consonant
  • Explore combined letters and clusters

🏆 Practice Tips

  • Write each letter multiple times
  • Say it aloud to practice pronunciation
  • Focus on ळ to distinguish Marathi from Sanskrit/Hindi
  • Look for the letters in simple everyday market words
  • Compare Marathi words with Sanskrit words to see differences
